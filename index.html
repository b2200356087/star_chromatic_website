Star Coloring of Hypergraphs

Abstract: This senior project investigates the star coloring problem for hypergraphs, extending classical graph coloring theory to higher-order structures. We establish both upper and lower bounds for the star chromatic number of r-uniform hypergraphs and provide probabilistic constructions demonstrating the tightness of these bounds.

Introduction

Hypergraph coloring has been extensively studied in the literature. The star-coloring of a hypergraph is defined as a vertex coloring without a bicolored (2-colored) star, where a star in a hypergraph is a collection of edges with a nonempty common intersection.

The star coloring problem for graphs was introduced by GrÃ¼nbaum, who proved that a graph with maximum degree 3 has an acyclic coloring with 4 colors. We extend these results to hypergraphs and establish tight bounds on the star chromatic number.

Definition: The star chromatic number of an $r$-uniform hypergraph $H$, denoted $\chi_s^r(H)$, is the minimum $k$ for which $H$ admits a star coloring with $k$ colors.

Upper Bound Results

Main Upper Bound Theorem

Let $H$ be an $r$-uniform hypergraph of maximum degree $\Delta$, with vertex-set $V=V(H)$ and edge-set $E = E(H)$. Then the star chromatic number satisfies:

$$\chi_s^r(H) \leq \left\lceil (2^{3r-3} \cdot 81 \cdot r^4 \cdot \alpha \cdot \Delta^3)^{\frac{2}{3r-4}} \right\rceil$$

for $r \geq 2$ and $\alpha = 3$.

Proof technique: We use the LovÃ¡sz Local Lemma with a careful analysis of dependency graphs. The proof involves defining "bad" events for monochromatic edges and bicolored non-star substructures, then showing these events can be avoided with positive probability.

Lower Bound Results

Main Lower Bound Theorem

If $\frac{d}{D} \geq \frac{2}{r}$, then:

$$\chi_s^r(d) \geq \frac{r-1}{r} d^{\frac{1}{r-1}}$$

Proof technique: We use the probabilistic method on ErdÅ‘s-RÃ©nyi random hypergraphs $G_r(n,p)$, showing that any coloring with too few colors must contain bichromatic triangles.

Key Supporting Results

Lemma (Linear Path)

For a real number $((\frac{r^2\ln(n)}{n} \cdot \frac{2^{r+3}}{3^{r-4}}))^{\frac{1}{3}} \leq p \leq 1$, let $m \leq \alpha(G_r(n,p))$ be the size of the maximum independent set in $G_r(n,p)$.

Then, asymptotically almost surely and uniformly over $p$, any coloring of $G_r(n,p)$ with $k \leq \frac{n}{r}$ colors, where each color class has at most $\alpha(G_r(n,p)) \leq (\frac{r^r\ln{n}}{p})^{\frac{1}{r-1}}$ vertices, contains a bichromatic triangle $C_3^r$.

Lemma (Independence Number)

Asymptotically almost surely:

$$\alpha(G_r(n,p)) \leq \left(\frac{r^r\ln{n}}{p}\right)^{\frac{1}{r-1}}$$

LovÃ¡sz Local Lemma (General Form)

Suppose that $G=(V,E)$ is a dependency graph for events $A_1,A_2,\ldots,A_n$ and suppose there are real numbers $y_1,y_2,\ldots,y_n$ such that $0 \leq y_i < 1$ and:

$$\Pr[A_i] \leq y_i \prod_{(i,j) \in E} (1-y_j) \quad \text{for all } 1 \leq i \leq n$$

Then $\Pr[\bigwedge_{i=1}^n \overline{A_i}] \geq \prod_{i=1}^n (1-y_i) > 0$.

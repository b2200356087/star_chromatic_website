Star Coloring of Hypergraphs

Abstract: This senior project investigates the star coloring problem for hypergraphs. We establish both upper and lower bounds for the star chromatic number of r-uniform hypergraphs. since the project is more theoretical in nature, and not application-oriented, a demo showcase is not possible.

Introduction

In this work we study hypergraphs, which generalize undirected graphs by allowing each edge to connect any number of vertices. Formally, an $r$-uniform hypergraph is a pair $H=(V,E)$ where each hyperedge $E\in E$ has exactly $r$ vertices.

We focus on the notion of star coloring for hypergraphs. A star coloring is a vertex coloring satisfying two conditions:

  1. No monochromatic edges:
  2. No bichromatic 3-paths except if the 3-path is also a star-path:

A star-path is a 3-path in which all three edges share at least one common vertex. No other 3-path, be it a triangle or a regular 3-path, may use only two colors.

Definition: The star chromatic number of an $r$-uniform hypergraph $H$, denoted $\chi_s^r(H)$, is the minimum number of colors $k$ for which $H$ admits a star coloring with $k$ colors.

The purpose of this research is to derive general upper and lower bounds on $\chi_s^r(H)$ for arbitrary $r$-uniform hypergraphs. To prove these bounds, we employ the probabilistic method: Where a random process is defined which randomly assigns certain properties to mathematical objects. Once it is shown that an object with a desired set of properties may be generated by this random process with non-zero probability, then we may conclude that such an object exists.

Upper Bound Results

Upper Bound

Let $H$ be an $r$-uniform hypergraph of maximum degree $\Delta$, with vertex-set $V=V(H)$ and edge-set $E = E(H)$. Then the star chromatic number satisfies:

$$\chi_s^r(H) \leq \left\lceil (2^{3r-3} \cdot 81 \cdot r^4 \cdot \alpha \cdot \Delta^3)^{\frac{2}{3r-4}} \right\rceil$$

for $r \geq 2$ and $\alpha = 3$.

Key supporting lemmas & proof sketch

General Lovász Local Lemma

\[ \Pr[A_i] \le y_i \prod_{(i,j)\in E}(1-y_j) \quad\text{for all }1 \le i \le n, \] where \(G=(V,E)\) is a dependency graph for the events \(A_1,\dots,A_n\) and \(0\le y_i<1\). Then \[ \Pr\!\bigl[\bigwedge_{i=1}^n \overline{A_i}\bigr] \;\ge\;\prod_{i=1}^n(1-y_i), \] so in particular there is positive probability that no \(A_i\) occurs.
Proof sketch.
  • Random coloring: Assign each vertex one of \(x = \left\lceil (2^{3r-3} \cdot 81 \cdot r^4 \cdot \alpha \cdot \Delta^3)^{\frac{2}{3r-4}} \right\rceil\) colors uniformly at random.
  • Bad event types:
    • AM: some edge is monochromatic.
    • Ai: a bicolored non-star 3-path of “size” \(3r-i\), for \(i=2,\dots,\tfrac{3r}{2}\).
  • Dependency graph: Events are adjacent whenever their underlying vertex-sets intersect. For example, two \(A_M\) events share an edge if the two hyperedges share any vertex.
  • Dependency table:
    Dependency table of bad events. Entry \([i,j]\) is an upper bound on how many events of type \(j\) a type \(i\) event depends on.
    M23…\(\tfrac{3r}{2}\)
    M \(r\Delta\) \(\tfrac{5}{2}r^3\Delta^3\) \(\tfrac{5}{2}r^3\Delta^3\) … \(\tfrac{5}{2}r^3\Delta^3\)
    2 \(3r\Delta\) \(\tfrac{15}{2}r^3\Delta^3\) \(\tfrac{15}{2}r^3\Delta^3\) … \(\tfrac{15}{2}r^3\Delta^3\)
    3 \(3r\Delta\) \(\tfrac{15}{2}r^3\Delta^3\) \(\tfrac{15}{2}r^3\Delta^3\) … \(\tfrac{15}{2}r^3\Delta^3\)
    ………………
    \(\tfrac{3r}{2}\) \(3r\Delta\) \(\tfrac{15}{2}r^3\Delta^3\) \(\tfrac{15}{2}r^3\Delta^3\) … \(\tfrac{15}{2}r^3\Delta^3\)
  • Apply the General Lovász Local Lemma:None of the bad events occur with positive probability; hence a star coloring with x colors exists.

Lower Bound Results

Lower Bound

$$\chi_s^r(d) \geq \frac{r-1}{r} d^{\frac{1}{r-1}}$$
Key supporting lemmas & proof sketch

Lemma (Independence Number)

Asymptotically almost surely:

$$\alpha(G_r(n,p)) \leq \left(\frac{r^r\ln{n}}{p}\right)^{\frac{1}{r-1}}$$

Lemma (Triangle)

For a real number $((\frac{r^2\ln(n)}{n} \cdot \frac{2^{r+3}}{3^{r-4}}))^{\frac{1}{3}} \leq p \leq 1$, let $m \leq \alpha(G_r(n,p))$ be the size of the maximum independent set in $G_r(n,p)$.

Then, asymptotically almost surely and uniformly over $p$, any coloring of $G_r(n,p)$ with $k \leq \frac{n}{r}$ colors, where each color class has at most $\alpha(G_r(n,p)) \leq (\frac{r^r\ln{n}}{p})^{\frac{1}{r-1}}$ vertices, contains a bichromatic triangle $C_3^r$.

It can also be shown that asymptotically almost surely \(\Delta(G(n,p)) \le d\).

Since each of these “good” events (small independence number, triangle existence, and bounded maximum degree) holds with probability tending to one, their intersection still has positive probability. Therefore all conditions can be satisfied simultaneously with nonzero probability.

Putting these together and rewriting in terms of \(\Delta\) yields the stated lower bound on \(\chi_s^r(d)\).
